MzUni & FAIR project

The computer centre, you are sitting in, is part of a collaboration between Mzuzu University and the Danish organization FAIR (FAIR Allocation of Infotech Resources). The purpose is to improve access to ICT and to strengthen education.

As

One of the main reasons that the project is possible and has an enormous potential, is that of open knowledge, open access, and open source. This is how Wikipedia, Khan Academy, and even the software and operating system which you are using, has been made freely available.

This centre was opened in September 2013.

Wikipedia

Wikipedia is a very large collection of articles, covering almost all areas of human knowledge. It is also said to be the largest single work of knowledge ever created by man. If you are looking for information, there is a good chance you can find it on Wikipedia - you can use the search function, or explore the wealth of topics by clicking on the links (blue text).

Wikipedia is created by people like you and me, from all over the world. The idea is that everyone has valuable knowledge, and this knowledge should be shared. Therefore everyone can write new articles, or edit the ones that are already there. In this way, people all over earth can share and use knowledge - maybe you know a lot about something? - By using the internet you can share this knowledge.

Our version Wikipedia is a copy that we have downloaded from the internet and placed on your shool's computer network. To add or edit articles you have to be connected to the Internet. Remember that knowledge can be shared in many other ways.

Khan Academy

Khan Academy is a video library, which contains hundreds of video lectures on Math and Science. It covers simple to advanced topics of Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics and many others. Remember that the videos contains sound, so you will need a pair of speakers or headphones.

Documentaries

Why Democracy and Why Poverty are documentary projects using film to start a global conversation about democracy and poverty. The movies will show you important subjects from around the world. Often the matters are so complex that it is difficult to say who/what is right and wrong.

Before watching a movie, you should find a pair of headphones and plug into the computer. Make sure that the sound is on. If you do not have time enough at the computer to watch the movie, maybe you can ask your teacher to screen it for you and your class mates.

In order to play a movie, click the picture of the movie. A program for playing movies will then start. The movies are just like DVDs, so first you will have to navigate a DVD menu and maybe add subtitles in English. Good luck!
